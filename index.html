About Me

I am currently an undergraduate student at Princeton University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering. My current research interests include quantum computing, information science, and optics. I also have a passion for programming, machine learning, and data analysis.

Beyond building fullstack websites, I enjoy experimenting with art and design, exploring creativity in all its forms!

Summer 2025

Girls Who Code Teaching Assistant

  • Taught game design principles and fundamentals during the 2025 Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program
  • Led and mentored groups of high school girls, guiding them through learning the basics of programming
  • Inspired students as a role model, fostering a sense of sisterhood and demonstrating how their projects can make a real-world impact.

2023-2024

Quantum Mechanics Researcher at Argonne National Laboratory

  • Studied multiple research papers on quantum entanglement and the geometric measurement of entanglement
  • Wrote a literature review summarizing the history and current understanding of entanglement measures for arbitrary two-qubit pure states, two-qubit mixed states, and certain multipartite mixed states.

2022-2023

Particle Physics Researcher at Argonne National Laboratory

  • Developed Monte Carlo simulations in Python using object-oriented design to model three potential factors influencing an unexpected seasonal variation in multiple muon events, and visualized results with Matplotlib
  • Compared and analyzed simulation outputs with particle detector data, identified one of three factors as the cause of seasonal variations, reinforced particle physicists’ hypothesis on muonbehavior, and concluded the study with a research paper
